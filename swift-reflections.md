**Week 1: 04/03/26**
I would define E-Literature as a piece of literature that is created for digital comsumption or intended to be consumed digitally. It can utilises tools otherwise unavailavle to create something that's impossible in the analog world. It can take advantage of hyperlinks/other digital tools to allow for consumer interaction. I think to be considered pure E-Literature is must contain some of the above elements, not simply be a scan of a script thats availbale for online download, or a book on a reading app. 

Of the examples, my favourite was probably First Draft of the Revolution - it felt like a fun mix of something very old and something very new. Actually I change my mind. Favourite might have been The kinetic poem "Taroko Gorge," as it felt like you're getting something unique and for your eyes only. Something traditional literature can't give.

I'm not too sure what to expect for this class. E-Literature is new to me. I've never created a piece of E-lit before. I think it's the future and it's possibilities excite me, so I'm very keen to learn more.

**Week 2: 12/03/2026:** In terms of ethics and AI, I think my personal stance holds very little weight. As AI becomes exponentially more advance and prevalent, the race against it becomes less and less level. It's a losing battle. It's AI bringing not just a gun, but a gun and a world of knowledge and tactics, to a knife fight. I think the only way forward is to co-exist with this omnipresent entity. If I had to give my opinion - I'm saddened by the idea of wholly human created art being obsolete. I do however understand this could simply be the fear of change. I imagine with the invention of the printing press, of computers, and the internet there were sceptics. Now these technologies are so intertwined with our lives and are that we couldn't imagine or produce either without them. Maybe there is potential for new art forms here. For new creation. For us to use AI as a aid, colleague, catalyst for something new. Something amazing. That is my hope. I will continue to educate myself around uses for this new tool, even if I'm sceptical, so that I am not left behind if in a few years we cannot imagine art without the use of AI.
